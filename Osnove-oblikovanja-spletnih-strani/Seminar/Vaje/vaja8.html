Many sites are available on the Web to help you find the information you need. These "search engines" search the entire Web looking for Web pages and other resources containing the words or phrases you enter into a search box. Four popular search sites are listed and linked below.


altavista

With AltaVista's search capabilities you can look for Web pages, graphic images, or video and audio files. You can build a query to search for all words, any words, or exact phrases, or you can enter Boolean expressions to further refine searches.


google

To enter a query into Google, just type in a few descriptive words and click on the Google search button for a list of relevant Web pages. Refining or narrowing the search is as simple as adding or subtracting words to the search terms.


lycos

To begin a simple Lycos search, type the word or phrase you are looking for into the text-entry box. Click the "Go" button to find all the links related to your search word or to words arranged on a search results page.


yahoo

Begin with the basic search box on the main Yahoo! page, or choose the ad-free start page at search.yahoo.com. You'll see links to other types of searches for directories, news stories, yellow pages, and images.

Other search sites are available and you can choose whichever gives you the best results. Certain sites can be set up as your default home page when opening your browser.
